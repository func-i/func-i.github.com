Functional Imperative

A social and public portal for sharing information about Canadian court decisions

This portal is the natural expansion of the Canadian Legal Information Institute’s core site which was visited by 9 out of 10 lawyers in 2011 and, in the same year, received nearly 7-million visits and delivered over 81 million page views. With one million documents across over 200 collections, CanLII is the platform lawyers and all Canadians use to consult Canadian law and Functional Imperative is building the platform to share and comment on this content.

Helping CanLII to develop a new online identity and a more social, relevant, and powerful platform

CanLII Connects will allow law firms, lawyers and academics, among other stakeholders, to associate opinions and summaries with relevant case law and legislation. Such content can then be voted and commented on by other CanLII users. CanLII Connects will integrate the views of Canada’s legal community into its most trusted source for legal information. It will highlight the greatest minds in Canada’s legal community and will facilitate access to the law

Future-proofing while offering a comparable experience for older browsers

In building CanLII Connects, we are leveraging our team’s strong investment in Ruby on Rails, HTML5, Sass and Coffee Script to deliver this project on time and under budget. With a focus on responsive first, we looked to use a number of cutting edge CSS3 technologies including CSS Regions, FlexBox and Columns, with a fallback to Javascript library Masonry.
