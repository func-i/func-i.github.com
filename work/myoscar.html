Functional Imperative

An online personal health record. Used by approximately 2,000 physicians

MyOSCAR is an online personal health record (PHR) that facilitates sharing of patient information from the Open Source Clinical Applications & Resources (OSCAR) system. The OSCAR electronic health record (EHR) system (also often referred to as an electronic medical record (EMR)) was originally created by McMaster University and has consistently been in the top three most used electronic medical record systems in Canada as it is currently used by approximately 2,000 physicians.

Direct access to over 3000 nurses, 1000 physicians and a patient population of over 1 million

Functional Imperative was subcontracted by the Healthcare Human Factors group at the University Health Network (UHN) based on their knowledge of Functional Imperative's technologies, healthcare experience, efficiency and professionalism. Healthcare Human Factors' world-class healthcare-focused usability, user experience and user interaction (UX/UI) research and recommendations are based on direct access to over 3000 nurses, 1000 physicians and a patient population of over 1 million.

MyOscar looked to thought-leaders in human centered design, usability and user experience and interaction to re-build their front-end. More specifically, our challenge was to redesign the existing MyOSCAR personal health record. This record can be informed by and shared with any health or wellness professional trusted by a user or patient.

Improved visibility, optimized navigation and supported patient workflow

Working closely with the Healthcare Human Factors group at UHN Functional Imperative undertook front-end development of the latest version of MyOSCAR. Through the use of human factors methodologies and iterative design, our redesign improved the visibility, optimized the navigation and now better supports patient workflow. This included the integration of complex designs into HTML5 and CSS layouts on short timelines. Multiple teams at hospitals and universities were involved including designers, developers, administrators, among others. Among other areas, Functional Imperative drove technology decisions related to Healthcare Human Factors's designs.
