Pomodoro Technique

How to work effectively without stress
by Michael Yagudaev on 12 Aug 2015

Pomodoro Technique

Over the last 18 months I have been using the Pomodoro technique. It all started by hearing of the basic premies at a meetup event.

The idea is simple, you write down a task you would like to accomplish and do 25 minutes of work. You then rest for 5 minutes, this is called a "pomodoro". Once you complete one pomodoro, you do it over again. After 4 pomodoros you take a longer 15 minute break.

The name came from those little kitchen timers shaped like tomatoes, in fact pomodoro means tomato in Italian.
